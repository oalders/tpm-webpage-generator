The Bid In Detail

Introduction

The biggest selling point to a YAPC::NA in Toronto is Toronto itself. The Greater Toronto Area has a population of around 6 million, and for the past decade the United Nations has ranked Toronto as the most multicultural city on Earth. It is remarkably vibrant on every dimension: the financial district bustles as one of the world's financial capitals; the array of ethnic neighbourhoods make you feel like you're visiting a continent, not a city; the street-life -- restaurants, shops, pedestrian concourses -- runs on for miles (well, kilometres); enough high culture and pop culture that you'd never run out.

The proposed facility, University of Toronto, St. Michael's College, is located in the heart of downtown. A large variety of shops and restaurants are withing easy walking distance. Convenient and easy public transport to major attractions is available. Renting a car is not necessary.

Currency Considerations

The Exchange Rate

  • The exchange rate is currently at about 1.38: one dollar US equals about $1.38 Canadian.

Using credit and debit cards

US Credit and debit cards generally Just Work. Most banks are on the Cirrus and Plus networks for debit cards.

  • Credit cards: You'll be billed your bank's US dollar equivalent to the CDN dollar amount of your purchase. Keep in mind that the exchange rate varies day to day.
  • Debit cards: You might want to confirm that your debit card works by making a withdrawal through some convenient ATM in Toronto. If your card works at the ATM, it should work as a debit card. (But note that it would have to be a very strange debit card for it not to work.)

Using bank cards for ATM (aka Bank Machine) withdrawals

  • You'll be offered various amounts in multiples of $20. Your total withdrawal, plus network charge, will be converted to US dollars and charged to your account.
  • Note that "white label" ATMs (found in convenience stores) charge an additional 'service charge'. Try to use a 'bank' ATM if you want to avoid paying for this service (recommended banks are CIBC, Royal, Scotiabank, TD/Canada Trust, Bank of Montreal).

Using USD$ within Canada

  • In most places of business you'll see some kind of exchange rate posted. The rate won't be as high as the official rate, so given an official rate of 1.38, you might see a rate of 1.25 or 1.30.
  • "U.S. money accepted at par" is not a good deal. :)
  • You can expect to get both American and Canadian coins as change; the exchange rate doesn't apply to coins.
  • If you plan to use US money in a taxi, be sure to negotiate the exchange rate before you start your trip.

Traveller's Cheques

  • Ask before you use them -- some businesses have a stamp for the 'Payee' section of the cheque. Make sure the person accepting the travellers cheques understands that it's US dollars and not CDN dollars.
  • They really aren't necessary in this day and age of ATM machines, though.
